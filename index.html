Why this exists

Most domain checkers are built around third-party APIs, rate limits and monthly fees. That is fine for one domain at a time, not when you are exploring whole wordlists for a product, studio or company. Domain Scout is designed for this: predictable performance, offline-friendly logic and full control over how you scan.

What Domain Scout actually does

Under the hood, Domain Scout combines DNS prefiltering with registry-level checks and a simple scoring layer:

• bulk scanning of large wordlists or generated names;

• dedicated handlers for .com/.net (Verisign), .it and .ch;

• filters for length, prefixes, suffixes and blacklist words;

• 0–100 score per free domain based on length, TLD value, readability;

• exports as TXT, CSV and Markdown for team decisions.

Who it is for

Domain Scout is aimed at people who have to ship, not just “check if a .com is free”:

• solo founders validating names before investing in branding;

• small studios and agencies doing naming work for clients;

• developers and makers who want a practical, script-friendly scanner.

FAQ

What is the fastest way to check domain availability?

For large lists, running a Domain Scout scan is usually faster and cheaper than calling a third-party API for every candidate. It filters with DNS first and then asks the registry only when needed.

Can I use my own wordlists?

Yes. Domain Scout is built around wordlists and simple configuration. You plug in your lists, apply blacklist rules and, if needed, generate brandable variations.

Is there a hosted version?

Domain Scout can run locally or on a VPS. A hosted version and paid plans can come later, using the same engine with automation and a dedicated UI.
